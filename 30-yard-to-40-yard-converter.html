Why the last 10 yards matter

Some athletes win the first 20 to 30 yards and level off. Others are average early but carry speed better later. That is why the result is an estimate, not a one-step conversion.

Best companion data

A 10-yard split helps most with early acceleration. Flying sprint data helps with whether the athlete is likely to carry speed into 40 yards.

Common mistake

Using pure linear math from 30 yards to 40 yards usually overstates precision because the extra 10 yards depend on how the athlete transitions into upright speed.
