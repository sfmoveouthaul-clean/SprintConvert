Average speed is not peak speed

If an athlete starts from stillness, average speed will always be lower than true top speed. That difference matters when comparing short sprint tests.

Helpful interpretation

Average speed answers "how fast did the whole effort go?" Estimated top speed answers "how fast did the athlete likely move at the fastest point?"

Benchmark note

This page still follows the same rule: no benchmark display unless a source-backed sport and profile match exists.

mph vs m/s

mph is easy to picture for many athletes and parents. m/s is often more useful when comparing splits, flying sprints, and track-style testing.

Why top speed may be blank

If the calculator does not have enough evidence from the sprint distance, flying split, or top-speed input, it avoids pretending to know the peak velocity exactly.
