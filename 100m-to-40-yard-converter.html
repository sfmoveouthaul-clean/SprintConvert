100m to 40 Yard Dash Converter

A 100m result can tell you something about sprint ability, but it does not map cleanly onto a short football-style sprint. This page keeps that difference front and center.

What changes when you convert from 100m

A 100m time already includes acceleration, upright speed, and speed endurance. A 40-yard result is much more acceleration-heavy.

Benchmark status

There is no general 100m benchmark dataset in v1, so the page shows only source-backed comparisons when another supported distance is available.

Why pure ratio math fails

An athlete who closes well in the last 40 meters of a 100m may not be the same athlete who wins the first 20 yards of a short sprint test.

What helps most

Top-speed and flying sprint data make the short-sprint estimate more useful because they show how much true upright speed the athlete has.
